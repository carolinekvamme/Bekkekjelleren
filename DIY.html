DIY - making my own wine cellar

I was forced to do something about all the wines stored and stacked everywhere at home and in my cabin in the mountains. So hard to keep track of all the wines I had too, so I finally decided to build what I wanted myself.

I googled and searched, I found producers that had different DIY-modules, very expensive, and not necessarily quite what I wanted either. I checked prices for planks in different qualities, and decided for oak which is solid, pretty and would give an exclusive background for my Premium Wines. I looked at many nice cellars online, I started drawing the modules and shelves I wanted and that would suit our needs, both for private wines and for tasting/sales via Bekkekjellerâ€™n.

A standard, small bedroom was sacrified. We decided to paint the walls in a nice grey tone, but it turned out darker then expected. In order to not have to do it all over, we decided to keep it, since all walls would be more or less covered with wines. I started with the corner shelves and the took both side walls with the shelves.

It really looked nice after a while! Wines were counted and arranged, systems were made, and house and cabin soon looked much tidier!
